# Security Policy

Kevv takes security reports for this repository and the official OpenHouse service seriously.

## Reporting a Vulnerability

Please do not open public GitHub issues for suspected vulnerabilities.

Send reports to `support@kevv.ai` with:

- a clear description of the issue
- reproduction steps or a proof of concept
- the affected commit, deployment, or environment if known
- any suggested mitigations or impact notes

Use a subject like `Security report: OpenHouse`.

## What to Expect

- Kevv will make a best effort to acknowledge receipt within 3 business days.
- Kevv will make a best effort to provide status updates during triage and remediation.
- Please give Kevv reasonable time to investigate and ship a fix before public disclosure.

## Scope

This policy covers:

- this public repository
- the official OpenHouse deployments operated by Kevv
- security issues in default integrations or configurations documented by Kevv

This policy does not guarantee support for:

- third-party forks not operated by Kevv
- custom deployments or modifications made by others
- social engineering, phishing, spam, or abuse reports without a concrete product vulnerability

## Safe Harbor

Kevv will not pursue legal action for good-faith security research that:

- avoids privacy violations, destructive actions, and service disruption
- does not access or exfiltrate user data beyond what is minimally necessary to demonstrate the issue
- does not exploit a vulnerability beyond what is necessary for proof of concept
- is promptly and privately reported to Kevv

## Sensitive Data

If you discover exposed credentials, tokens, personal data, or customer information, stop testing and report the issue immediately. Do not copy, retain, share, or further process sensitive data except as strictly necessary to report the issue responsibly.
